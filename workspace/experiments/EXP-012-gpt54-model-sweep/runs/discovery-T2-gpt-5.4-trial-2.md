## Problem / Opportunity
**Improve the onboarding experience for new business customers.**

## Discovery framing
This is a broad opportunity area, so the next step is usually to make it more specific. “Onboarding” could include things like:

- Account setup
- Verification / compliance steps
- Product configuration
- Data migration or integrations
- First-time user education
- Internal handoff from sales to customer success
- Time to first value
- Early retention / activation

## Helpful clarification questions
To shape this into a strong discovery target, I’d want to know:

1. **What type of business customers?**
   - SMB, mid-market, enterprise?
   - Any specific industries or segments?

2. **What product or service are they onboarding into?**
   - SaaS platform, fintech product, marketplace, services offering, etc.

3. **What part of onboarding feels broken today?**
   - Drop-off
   - Slow setup
   - Confusing steps
   - Too much manual support
   - Low activation
   - Poor conversion from signed to active

4. **What signals suggest there’s a problem?**
   - Support tickets
   - Low completion rates
   - Long time-to-value
   - Customer complaints
   - Lost revenue
   - CSM/sales feedback

5. **What outcome matters most?**
   - Faster activation
   - Higher completion
   - Better retention
   - Lower support cost
   - Higher NPS/CSAT
   - More expansion potential

## Example problem statements
Depending on your goal, this could become:

- **New business customers are taking too long to reach first value during onboarding.**
- **Too many new business customers drop off before completing onboarding.**
- **The onboarding journey for business customers is too manual and resource-intensive.**
- **New business customers are confused by setup requirements, leading to delays and support burden.**
- **The current onboarding experience does not adapt well to different business customer segments.**

If you want a solid starting point for discovery, I’d phrase it as:

**How might we help new business customers complete onboarding faster and reach first value with less friction?**
