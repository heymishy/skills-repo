## Discovery summary for loan ledger migration

### Situation
You are decommissioning a legacy enterprise loan ledger that has been in production for 18 years. It currently manages about **280,000 active loan accounts** across personal, home, and commercial lending.

A **new core banking platform** is already live for deposit accounts and has been operating successfully for **14 months**. The goal is to migrate all loan accounts onto that same platform, retire the legacy system, and end up with **one core banking platform**.

### Hard constraints
- **Legacy vendor support ends in 12 months**
- After that:
  - no patches
  - no security updates
  - no vendor support
- Extension is **not possible**
- Therefore, the **12-month window is the absolute cutover deadline**

### Planned migration approach
- **6-month parallel run**
  - New loan originations go to the new platform from day one
  - Existing loans remain on the legacy system in shadow mode
  - All transactions are mirrored to the new platform
- At month 6:
  - validate data integrity
  - perform reconciliation
  - if acceptable, cut over remaining legacy accounts in a staged weekend migration
- Then:
  - **6 more months to decommission** the legacy infrastructure formally

---

## Key business and technical requirements

### 1) Data integrity is the primary risk
You must migrate:
- all **280,000 loan records**
- full loan history, including:
  - original loan terms
  - drawdown history
  - repayment schedules
  - arrears status
  - associated security registrations

Critical requirement:
- **No loan record can be lost or corrupted**
- Closed loans must still be preserved because of legal retention requirements

### 2) Records retention
Legal advice confirms:
- credit contract records must be retained for **7 years**
- this applies even to **closed loans**
- migrated historical records must remain accessible and preserved for compliance

### 3) Regulatory reporting
The new platform must produce all **RBNZ regulatory reports** currently generated by the legacy system.

Requirements:
- prescribed report formats and field definitions must be followed
- the **RBNZ relationship team** must be involved to confirm the outputs meet regulatory expectations

### 4) Delivery model and resourcing
- Project will be **run in-house**
- The new platform vendor will provide a **migration toolset**
- Expected team:
  - **6 engineers**
  - **1 data architect**
  - **1 project manager**

---

## Primary risks
1. **Data migration defects**
   - loss of historical transactions
   - mismatched repayment schedules
   - incorrect arrears balances
   - broken security references

2. **Reconciliation failure during parallel run**
   - mirrored transactions may diverge between systems
   - cutover may be delayed if integrity checks fail

3. **Regulatory reporting mismatch**
   - RBNZ reports may differ in field mapping, timing, or calculations

4. **Retirement deadline pressure**
   - only 12 months until unsupported legacy system
   - 6 months of parallel run leaves limited time for remediation

5. **Retention and auditability**
   - old and migrated records must remain retrievable for 7 years
   - audit trail integrity is essential

---

## Important implications
- This is not just a data move; it is a **system-of-record transition**
- The migration must preserve:
  - contractual history
  - financial correctness
  - audit trails
  - regulatory outputs
- The parallel-run design is good for risk reduction, but it creates a need for:
  - rigorous transaction mirroring
  - reconciliation tooling
  - exception management
  - clear cutover criteria

## Likely discovery outputs / next steps
To move into planning, you will likely need to confirm:

### Functional scope
- Which loan products are in scope?
- Are all lending products already supported on the new platform?
- Are there product-specific rules, fees, or interest calculations that need custom handling?

### Data scope
- What exact entities must be migrated?
- What historic depth is required for:
  - transactions
  - statements
  - documents
  - security registrations
- What must remain online vs archived?

### Reporting scope
- Which RBNZ reports are produced today?
- What transformation or mapping is needed?
- What sign-off process will RBNZ relationship team use?

### Cutover and operations
- What defines “satisfactory” reconciliation?
- What are the rollback criteria?
- How will staged weekend migration be executed?
- What’s the operational support model during parallel run?

### Compliance and retention
- What archive/storage solution will hold records for 7 years?
- How will access, audit, and retrieval be maintained after decommission?

## Assessment
This is a **high-risk, time-constrained migration** with strong compliance implications. The schedule is feasible only if:
- data mapping is highly controlled
- reporting equivalence is validated early
- reconciliation is automated
- the team has clear cutover and remediation criteria
