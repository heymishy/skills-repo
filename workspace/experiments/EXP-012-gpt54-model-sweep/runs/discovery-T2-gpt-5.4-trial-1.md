Thanks — you want to explore:

**Improving the onboarding experience for new business customers.**

To kick off discovery, I’d frame the opportunity like this:

### Initial opportunity statement
How might we help **new business customers reach first value faster and with less friction**, so they successfully activate, adopt the product, and remain engaged?

### Good next discovery angles
We’d usually want to clarify:
1. **Who exactly are “new business customers”?**
   - SMB, mid-market, enterprise?
   - Admin/buyer vs end users?
   - Self-serve vs sales-led?

2. **What does onboarding include today?**
   - Signup
   - Account setup
   - Integrations
   - Data migration
   - User invites
   - Training / enablement
   - Procurement / security review

3. **Where is the pain showing up?**
   - Drop-off during setup
   - Long time-to-value
   - Low activation rates
   - Poor handoff from sales to CS
   - Too many manual steps
   - Confusing product configuration
   - Low adoption after initial launch

4. **What outcome matters most?**
   - Higher activation
   - Faster go-live
   - More seats adopted
   - Better retention
   - Lower onboarding/support cost
   - Higher customer satisfaction

### Discovery starter hypothesis
A useful starting hypothesis could be:

> New business customers struggle during onboarding because the process is fragmented, unclear, and too effortful, causing delays to first value and reducing activation and long-term adoption.

### Recommended next step
The next best move is to narrow the problem by answering:

**Which specific part of business customer onboarding is currently underperforming, and how do you know?**
