## Problem Statement

The enterprise loses approximately NZD $4.2M annually in retail trans-Tasman payment revenue to alternative services — Wise, Western Union, and similar remittance providers — because the enterprise's SWIFT-based international payment product is too slow (1–2 business days) and too expensive ($18–25 per transaction) for the price-sensitive consumer market. A proprietary intra-group routing channel, using the enterprise's existing group relationship with the enterprise's Australian counterpart, offers a technically credible path to sub-$5 pricing and 2-hour settlement for NZ-to-AU payments up to $10,000.

The opportunity is real and the competitive argument is sound. However, bringing a new cross-border payment channel to market in a dual-jurisdiction regulated environment — with a novel intra-group net settlement model and a significant change to how NZD/AUD payment volume currently flows through the enterprise's correspondent banking infrastructure — involves a series of regulatory, contractual, and operational obligations that the brief materially understates.

The core challenge is multi-dimensional:

**NZ regulatory leg (RBNZ AML/CFT + RBNZ FX reporting + DIA):** The enterprise retains primary AML/CFT liability under the RBNZ AML/CFT Act 2009 for every payment instruction it originates on behalf of customers — regardless of routing channel. The enterprise's statement that "our compliance team is comfortable" describes the existing SWIFT-channel screening posture, not a reviewed and confirmed analysis of the new intra-group channel. The AML/CFT obligations are channel-independent: sanctions screening must apply to every intra-group-routed instruction on equivalent terms to SWIFT-routed instructions, and RBNZ threshold transaction reporting (payments above NZD $10,000) applies regardless of whether settlement occurs via SWIFT or via internal group books. Additionally, the end-of-day NZD/AUD net settlement of intra-group positions is a foreign exchange transaction — the RBNZ FX Transaction Reporting obligation must be assessed for the net settlement model specifically, as the existing per-payment FX reporting infrastructure was designed for SWIFT transactions, not intra-group net settlement. Finally, the new proprietary channel with its novel settlement model may constitute a new payment service type under the Payment Services Regulations 2021, requiring DIA registration before the service can be offered to retail customers.

**Australian regulatory leg (AUSTRAC):** The brief presents AU-side regulatory compliance as the enterprise's Australian counterpart's problem. It is not the enterprise's Australian counterpart's problem alone. AUSTRAC's Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (Cth) creates a direct information-provision obligation on the sending entity: the enterprise must provide sufficient originator information with each credit instruction to enable the enterprise's Australian counterpart to satisfy its AUSTRAC correspondent banking KYC and record retention obligations. The minimum required fields — full legal name, account number, address, purpose — and their format must be confirmed with the enterprise's Australian counterpart compliance team before the channel goes live. The enterprise's Australian counterpart's AML/CTF Programme must document the intra-group arrangement as a correspondent banking relationship, and due diligence on the enterprise as the originating entity must be completed before the arrangement operates. A group ownership relationship does not exempt this arrangement from AUSTRAC's programme requirements. Inadequate originator information provision may cause the enterprise's Australian counterpart to delay or reject credit instructions, directly undermining the 2-hour settlement SLA commitment.

**Correspondent banking infrastructure (contractual):** The enterprise routes NZD/AUD international payments through a SWIFT correspondent relationship with JPMorgan Chase. The proposed proprietary channel redirects eligible NZD/AUD payment volume away from this correspondent relationship. Architecture guardrail ADR-CB-002 requires that any change to international payment routing include a review of all active correspondent bank agreements for the affected corridors to confirm the proposed routing is not restricted by existing terms. ADR-CB-002 notes specifically that correspondent bank agreements commonly contain terms governing routing of transactions through the correspondent, and that routing changes bypassing a SWIFT correspondent without satisfying any notification obligation in the relevant agreement create contractual and credit relationship risk. The TTPS-RISK-001 registry entry confirms the impact of the proprietary channel on the JPMorgan Chase correspondent relationship has not been assessed. This review is a mandatory pre-launch prerequisite: the bilateral correspondent banking agreement with JPMorgan Chase must be reviewed by Treasury Legal before any NZD/AUD customer transactions are routed through the proprietary channel.

The problem is therefore not simply "build a fast payment channel." It is: build a fast payment channel while satisfying dual-jurisdiction AML/CFT obligations (RBNZ and AUSTRAC), confirming RBNZ FX reporting obligations for the net settlement model, obtaining DIA registration or licence confirmation, and reviewing and satisfying the correspondent banking agreement obligations before the channel handles any customer transactions.

## Who It Affects

**The enterprise retail customers (primary beneficiary):** NZ customers with recurring trans-Tasman payment needs — family remittances, small business payments, regular transfers to Australian accounts. Benefit: materially lower transaction cost and faster settlement than the current SWIFT product.

**Payments product team:** Owns the feature. Responsible for ensuring all regulatory and contractual pre-conditions are satisfied before retail launch. Must engage Treasury Legal, Regulatory Affairs, RBNZ AML/CFT Compliance, and the enterprise's Australian counterpart compliance teams before committing build scope.

**RBNZ AML/CFT compliance team (NZ):** Owns the NZ-leg screening obligations. Must produce a written confirmation that the existing AML/CFT screening service covers intra-group-routed instructions on equivalent terms to SWIFT-routed instructions. Must confirm RBNZ threshold transaction reporting infrastructure covers intra-group channel transactions.

**The enterprise treasury team (NZ):** Owns the net settlement FX position management. Must confirm RBNZ FX transaction reporting obligations for the end-of-day NZD/AUD net settlement model, and whether the existing FX reporting infrastructure covers net settlement positions.

**AUSTRAC (Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre):** Governs the inbound AU-leg. Does not interact with the enterprise directly — but governs the enterprise's Australian counterpart, and the enterprise's AUSTRAC exposure is through its obligation to provide adequate originator information to the enterprise's Australian counterpart.

**The enterprise's Australian counterpart treasury and compliance teams (settlement partner):** Receive credit instructions from the enterprise via the intra-group channel. Must confirm originator information format and content requirements under their AUSTRAC programme. Must update their AML/CTF Programme to document the intra-group arrangement as a correspondent banking relationship and conduct enterprise due diligence before the channel operates.

**DIA (Department of Internal Affairs — NZ):** Payment service registration authority. Regulatory Affairs team must obtain written DIA confirmation of whether the new proprietary channel constitutes a new payment service type and whether additional DIA registration is required before retail launch.

**JPMorgan Chase — SWIFT correspondent bank (contractual):** The enterprise's active SWIFT correspondent for NZD/AUD international payments. Architecture guardrail ADR-CB-002 and EA registry TTPS-RISK-001 identify the impact on this correspondent relationship as not assessed and requiring review. Treasury Legal must review the bilateral correspondent banking agreement to determine whether prior written notification or consent is required before NZD/AUD transactions are redirected through the proprietary channel.

## MVP Scope

The minimum deliverable that enables NZ-to-AU retail customer payment transfers via the proprietary intra-group channel with full dual-jurisdiction AML/CFT compliance, and all contractual and regulatory pre-conditions met:

1. **Payment instruction intake and threshold routing** — accept NZ retail customer payment instructions for AU beneficiaries from the Retail Digital Banking Platform (TTPS-IN-001) and Phone Banking System (TTPS-IN-002). Route instructions above NZD $10,000 to the existing SWIFT gateway (TTPS-SWIFT-001) with standard pricing. Route eligible instructions (≤NZD $10,000, AU beneficiary, AML/CFT screening passed) to the proprietary intra-group channel.

2. **AML/CFT sanctions screening — intra-group channel** — all payment instructions routed via the intra-group channel must pass through the AML/CFT Sanctions Screening Service (TTPS-SCR-001) synchronously before the instruction is committed to the intra-group settlement. Screening lists: RBNZ-designated persons, OFAC SDN, DFAT consolidated (AU beneficiary). Fallback behaviour: screening service unavailable → decline instruction; do not proceed to settlement (per ADR-CB-004). The existing screening service integration must be explicitly validated for intra-group channel instructions — a new integration test is required, not an assumption of coverage from the SWIFT flow.

3. **RBNZ threshold transaction reporting — intra-group channel** — the existing RBNZ threshold transaction reporting infrastructure (TTPS-REP-001) must be confirmed as covering intra-group-routed transactions above NZD $10,000 on the same terms as SWIFT-routed transactions. If the reporting infrastructure is channel-specific to the SWIFT flow, an integration extension is required before go-live.

4. **AUSTRAC-compliant originator information provision** — the credit instruction sent to the enterprise's Australian counterpart via the intra-group channel must include the full originator information bundle required by AUSTRAC: originating customer's full legal name, account number, NZ address, and purpose of payment. The exact field format and content requirements must be confirmed with the enterprise's Australian counterpart compliance team before go-live. The intra-group channel's payment instruction data model must carry these fields end-to-end from customer intake to the credit instruction at TTPS-REG-002.

5. **Net settlement position management and FX reporting** — end-of-day NZD/AUD net settlement positions are transmitted to the enterprise group Treasury Net Settlement service (TTPS-SET-001). RBNZ FX transaction reporting obligations for intra-group net positions must be confirmed with the enterprise treasury team before go-live. If the existing FX reporting infrastructure does not cover net settlement positions, reporting logic extensions are in scope.

6. **Customer payment confirmation** — settlement confirmation message sent to the originating customer via TTPS-CONF-001 within the 2-hour SLA window.

**Pre-launch pre-conditions (must be resolved before the channel handles any customer transactions — not in engineering scope but tracked as delivery blockers):**
- B1/C1 gate: RBNZ AML/CFT — written compliance confirmation that the existing AML/CFT screening service covers intra-group-routed instructions on equivalent terms to SWIFT-routed instructions; RBNZ threshold transaction reporting infrastructure confirmed for intra-group transactions.
- B2/C2 gate: AUSTRAC — originator information field format and content confirmed with the enterprise's Australian counterpart compliance team; the enterprise's Australian counterpart AML/CTF Programme updated to document the intra-group correspondent banking arrangement; enterprise's Australian counterpart due diligence on the enterprise completed before channel activation.
- B3/C3 gate: RBNZ FX transaction reporting — treasury team to produce written confirmation of net settlement reporting obligations under RBNZ FX rules; reporting infrastructure confirmed to cover intra-group net positions, or extensions commissioned.
- B4/C4 gate: DIA registration — Regulatory Affairs team to obtain written DIA assessment of whether the new proprietary channel constitutes a new payment service type; either confirms existing licence coverage or initiates DIA registration. Channel may not be offered to retail customers before DIA confirmation.
- B5/C5 gate: Correspondent bank agreement review — Treasury Legal to review the bilateral correspondent banking agreement with JPMorgan Chase; confirm whether prior written notification or consent is required before NZD/AUD transactions are routed via the proprietary intra-group channel; any required notification to be filed and acknowledged before channel activation.

## Out of Scope

1. **AU-to-NZ payment direction** — inbound payments from Australian customers to NZ accounts are a future phase. This MVP covers NZ-to-AU only.
2. **Payments above NZD $10,000 via proprietary channel** — standard SWIFT channel with existing pricing and processing applies above the threshold.
3. **International corridors outside Australia** — the proprietary intra-group channel covers NZD/AUD payments only. All other international corridors remain on the standard SWIFT channel.
4. **AML/CFT framework redesign** — the enterprise retains and extends its existing RBNZ AML/CFT screening service. MVP scope is integration and validation of the existing service for the new channel, not a redesign of the AML/CFT programme.
5. **Enterprise's Australian counterpart-side system development** — the enterprise's scope ends at the credit instruction sent via the intra-group API. The enterprise's Australian counterpart's systems for receiving credit instructions and crediting Australian beneficiary accounts are out of scope.
6. **JPMorgan Chase correspondent relationship commercial management** — managing the commercial implications of reduced NZD/AUD transaction volume flowing through the SWIFT correspondent relationship is a Treasury matter outside product engineering scope. It is, however, a required pre-launch pre-condition (B5/C5): any notification or consent obligations arising from the bilateral agreement must be satisfied before channel activation.
7. **Retrospective payment volume migration** — historical SWIFT payments are not impacted. The proprietary channel applies to new instructions from the go-live date.
8. **Automated submission or settlement without human confirmation** — for payment instructions that fail AML/CFT screening or AUSTRAC information validation, no automatic override or retry path is in scope. Declined instructions are returned to the customer interface with an error state.

## Assumptions and Risks

### Assumptions

[ASSUMPTION — A1 — AML/CFT screening coverage applies to intra-group channel] The existing AML/CFT Sanctions Screening Service (TTPS-SCR-001) provides equivalent coverage for intra-group-routed payment instructions as for SWIFT-routed instructions, with no channel-specific configuration gaps. If the screening service has channel-specific integration logic tied to the SWIFT flow that does not apply to the intra-group channel, additional integration work is required. This assumption must be validated by the compliance team with a written confirmation before B1/C1 gate is cleared.

[ASSUMPTION — A2 — AUSTRAC originator information requirements can be met within the payment instruction data model] The originator information fields required by the enterprise's Australian counterpart's AUSTRAC programme (full legal name, account number, NZ address, purpose) are already held by the enterprise within its customer account records and can be included in the credit instruction to the enterprise's Australian counterpart without additional customer-facing data collection. If AUSTRAC or the enterprise's Australian counterpart's programme requires fields not currently held (e.g. national identity document number for non-account customers), customer intake changes may be required.

[ASSUMPTION — A3 — DIA registration or licence confirmation obtainable within delivery timeline] DIA confirms either: (a) the new proprietary channel is covered by the enterprise's existing payment service licence; or (b) DIA registration is required, and the registration process can be completed within the 6-month build timeline. If DIA registration is required and approval extends beyond the build timeline, the retail launch is deferred regardless of technical completion.

[ASSUMPTION — A4 — Net settlement positions reportable under RBNZ FX rules without per-transaction breakdown] The treasury team confirms that end-of-day NZD/AUD net settlement positions can be reported as a single daily net FX transaction (or confirmed as below reporting threshold) rather than requiring per-payment transaction-level FX reporting. If RBNZ FX rules require individual transaction-level reporting for each underlying payment, the net settlement model may need to be restructured.

### Blockers

[BLOCKER — B1 — AML/CFT screening validation required for intra-group channel before activation] The brief asserts the existing AML/CFT screening process covers requirements — but this assertion has not been validated for a non-SWIFT intra-group channel. RBNZ AML/CFT obligations are explicit: the screening obligation attaches to the payment instruction, not the transmission channel (policy doc s.A.3.2). The enterprise retains primary AML/CFT liability regardless of routing (s.A.1). Before the first customer instruction is routed via the intra-group channel, the compliance team must produce a written confirmation that the existing screening service integration provides equivalent pre-transmission screening for intra-group-routed instructions, including all mandatory screening lists. The RBNZ threshold transaction reporting module (TTPS-REP-001) must also be confirmed as covering intra-group transactions. **Go-live is blocked on written compliance confirmation addressing both AML/CFT screening coverage and threshold reporting coverage for the intra-group channel.**

[BLOCKER — B2 — AUSTRAC originator information requirements must be confirmed with the enterprise's Australian counterpart before channel activation] The brief treats AU-side compliance as the enterprise's Australian counterpart's responsibility. AUSTRAC's correspondent banking obligations impose a direct information-provision obligation on the enterprise as the sending entity: the enterprise must provide sufficient originator information in each credit instruction for the enterprise's Australian counterpart to satisfy its AUSTRAC KYC obligations. "Our compliance team is comfortable" covers RBNZ AML/CFT on the NZ side — it does not address AUSTRAC. The enterprise's Australian counterpart compliance team has not confirmed the required format or content. An inadequate originator information bundle may cause the enterprise's Australian counterpart to delay or reject credit instructions under its AUSTRAC programme, directly breaching the 2-hour settlement SLA. Additionally, the enterprise's Australian counterpart must update its AML/CTF Programme to document the intra-group arrangement as a correspondent banking relationship and complete due diligence on the enterprise. **Both the originator information format confirmation and the AML/CTF Programme update are mandatory pre-launch gates.**

[BLOCKER — B3 — RBNZ FX transaction reporting for net settlement model not confirmed] The net settlement model — end-of-day NZD/AUD net positions settled in group treasury books — is a foreign exchange transaction for RBNZ FX Transaction Reporting purposes. The existing FX reporting infrastructure was designed for SWIFT per-payment reporting; its coverage of intra-group net settlement positions has not been confirmed (EA registry TTPS-RISK-003). This must be resolved before go-live. **Build work on settlement infrastructure may proceed, but the net settlement model may not process live customer transactions before the treasury team produces written confirmation of RBNZ FX reporting compliance.**

[BLOCKER — B4 — DIA payment service type registration status not confirmed] The Payment Services Regulations 2021 requires DIA registration for new payment service types. The proprietary intra-group channel — with its novel settlement model, intra-group counterparty, and sub-$10,000 pricing threshold — may constitute a new payment service type not covered by the enterprise's existing payment service licence (architecture guardrail ADR-CB-007). DIA written confirmation must be obtained from the Regulatory Affairs team. **The retail launch is blocked on DIA written assessment, whether that confirms existing licence coverage or initiates new type registration.**

[RISK — R1 — Correspondent bank agreement review: JPMorgan Chase (escalate to BLOCKER if notification obligation confirmed)] The enterprise routes NZD/AUD international payments through a SWIFT correspondent relationship with JPMorgan Chase. The proposed proprietary channel redirects eligible NZD/AUD payment volume away from this SWIFT correspondent relationship. Architecture guardrail ADR-CB-002 requires a review of all active correspondent bank agreements for affected corridors before any routing change is implemented. ADR-CB-002 notes that correspondent bank agreements may contain notification obligations for routing changes, and that bypassing a SWIFT correspondent without satisfying such obligations creates contractual and credit relationship risk. EA registry TTPS-RISK-001 (severity: HIGH) confirms the impact of the proprietary channel on the JPMorgan Chase correspondent relationship has not been assessed. **Treasury Legal must review the bilateral correspondent banking agreement with JPMorgan Chase to determine whether prior written notification or consent is required before NZD/AUD transactions are routed through the proprietary channel.** This review must be completed before channel design is finalised. If the agreement review confirms a notification obligation exists, this risk is elevated to a BLOCKER until the notification is filed and acknowledged.

## Success Indicators

1. Customer-facing settlement: NZ-to-AU payments up to NZD $10,000 settled within 2 hours for eligible instructions (AU beneficiary, AML/CFT screening passed, AUSTRAC originator information fields complete).
2. Transaction fee: ≤NZD $5 per eligible transaction. Baseline: NZD $18–25 via standard SWIFT.
3. Annual payment revenue impact: NZD $1M+ recovered from alternative services within 12 months of full retail rollout (incremental progress towards the NZD $4.2M annual gap).
4. Dual-jurisdiction AML/CFT compliance: zero RBNZ or AUSTRAC compliance findings related to the intra-group channel in the first 12 months post-launch.
5. AUSTRAC originator information compliance: zero instances of the enterprise's Australian counterpart delaying or rejecting credit instructions due to inadequate originator information in the first 12 months.
6. DIA confirmation: written DIA assessment obtained before retail launch; new service type registration (if required) completed before launch.
7. RBNZ FX transaction reporting: treasury team written confirmation of net settlement reporting obligations obtained before go-live; RBNZ FX reporting infrastructure confirmed to cover intra-group net positions.
8. Correspondent bank agreement: Treasury Legal review of JPMorgan Chase bilateral agreement completed before channel design is finalised; any required notification or consent process completed and acknowledged before channel activation; zero compliance incidents with JPMorgan Chase arising from the routing change.
