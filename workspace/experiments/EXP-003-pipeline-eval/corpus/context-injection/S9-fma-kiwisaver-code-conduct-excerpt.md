# FMA KiwiSaver Code of Conduct — Selected Excerpts (2023 Edition)
## Relevant to: Significant Event Notices, Member Notification, PIE Manager Obligations
### (Simulated for EXP-003 S9 evaluation)

---

## Part 1 — Purpose and Application

This Code sets out the standards the FMA expects KiwiSaver scheme managers and trustees to meet in their dealings with members. It applies to all registered KiwiSaver schemes operating under the KiwiSaver Act 2006 and the Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013.

Scheme managers and trustees are expected to act in the best interests of members and to maintain the member experience standards set out in this Code at all times. Where a scheme manager proposes to make a material change to a member-facing product, process, or service, the scheme manager must follow the Significant Event Notice process set out in Part 3 of this Code.

---

## Part 2 — Member Communication Standards

**2.1 Clarity and accessibility**

All member communications must be written in plain language, tested for clarity with representative members, and accessible to members who use screen readers or other assistive technologies. Communications must be made available in Te Reo Māori on request.

**2.2 Switching process communications**

Where a member submits a fund switching instruction, the scheme manager must:
(a) Confirm receipt of the instruction in writing (email or SMS acceptable) within one business day;
(b) Confirm the effective date of the switch — the date from which the member's balance will be invested in the target fund;
(c) Disclose the applicable unit price date for the switch (i.e., the pricing date used to calculate the unit allocation);
(d) Disclose any applicable switching fee or waiver, in plain language, in the confirmation message;
(e) Provide the member with a reference number for the instruction.

**2.3 Rejection communications**

Where a switch instruction is rejected for any reason (including eligibility restrictions under the KiwiSaver Act), the scheme manager must inform the member of the rejection reason in plain language and provide information about how the member can switch by an alternative method (e.g., paper form or assisted channel).

## Part 3 — Significant Event Notice Obligations

**3.1 What is a Significant Event**

A Significant Event is any change to a KiwiSaver scheme's features, terms, or member-facing processes that a reasonable member would consider material to their decision to remain in the scheme or to the way in which they manage their account.

The following are examples of events that are always treated as Significant Events:
(a) Introducing a new fee or charge, or increasing an existing fee or charge;
(b) Adding a new fund option or removing an existing fund option;
(c) Changing the processing timeline for fund switching instructions (including moving from paper to digital, or from digital to paper);
(d) Changing the unit pricing methodology;
(e) Changing the default fund allocation for new members.

The introduction of an online digital switching capability where no such capability previously existed is a material change to the switching process under section 3.1(c) and therefore constitutes a Significant Event.

**3.2 Filing requirements**

The scheme manager must file a Significant Event Notice (SEN) with the FMA before making the change. The SEN must include:
(a) A description of the proposed change and its effective date;
(b) The reason for the change;
(c) An explanation of how the change affects members;
(d) A copy of the member communication the scheme manager proposes to send.

The SEN must be filed using the FMA's electronic filing portal. The FMA may respond to the SEN within the notice period with questions or requests for additional information. The scheme manager must not make the change until the notice period has expired.

**3.3 Member notification — 30-day advance notice requirement**

In addition to filing the SEN with the FMA, the scheme manager must give members advance notice of the change. Members must be notified at least **30 days before the change takes effect**.

The 30-day period begins on the date the member notification is sent, not the date the SEN is filed with the FMA. Where the FMA responds to the SEN with questions, the member notification period continues to run, but the scheme manager must not implement the change until both the 30-day member notice period has expired and any FMA queries have been resolved.

The member notification must:
(a) Be sent to each member via the member's preferred communication channel (email or post);
(b) Describe the change in plain language;
(c) State the effective date of the change;
(d) Invite members to contact the scheme manager if they have questions;
(e) Be accompanied by, or include a link to, the scheme's updated disclosure document where relevant.

**3.4 Consequences of non-compliance**

Implementing a Significant Event without filing a SEN or without completing the 30-day member notification period constitutes a breach of the scheme's licence conditions under the Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013. The FMA may:
(a) Require the scheme manager to notify members retrospectively and postpone the effective date of the change;
(b) Issue a formal warning;
(c) Impose conditions on the scheme's licence;
(d) Consider the breach in the scheme manager's supervisory risk assessment.

## Part 4 — PIE Manager Obligations for Material Product Changes

**4.1 Disclosure obligations**

Where a scheme manager introduces a new process or feature that results in a change to the scheme's Product Disclosure Statement (PDS) or Fund Updates, the scheme manager must:
(a) Update the PDS and Fund Updates before the change takes effect;
(b) File the updated disclosure documents on the FMA's offers register;
(c) Ensure members can access the updated disclosure documents from the member portal.

**4.2 Fee changes**

Where a fee change is proposed (including introducing a new switching fee), the scheme manager must:
(a) File an amended PDS with the FMA at least 20 business days before the fee change takes effect;
(b) Send individual member notification consistent with Part 3 of this Code;
(c) Ensure the fee is clearly disclosed in the confirmation message for any transaction to which the fee applies.

The FMA expects that fee changes are accompanied by a clear explanation of the fee structure, including how the fee is calculated, when it applies, and any applicable waivers. Disclosure of waiver conditions must be complete — partial disclosure that omits applicable waivers does not meet the FMA's standard.

*Note: Section 4.2 requires disclosure of applicable waivers. The scheme manager is responsible for ensuring that all statutory waivers applicable to the fee are documented and disclosed. The FMA does not consider "operations handles it manually" to be an adequate disclosure of a waiver that is required by statute.*

## Part 5 — Processing Timeline and Service Standards

**5.1 Fund switching processing standard**

The FMA expects that scheme managers process fund switching instructions promptly. The FMA's service expectation, consistent with KiwiSaver Act s.45, is that a valid switch instruction received before the fund's daily cut-off time is given effect at the fund's next available pricing date.

For most funds, the daily cut-off time is 3:00 pm on business days. Instructions received after the cut-off are given effect at the following business day's pricing date.

A digital switching capability that introduces delays beyond the statutory standard (e.g., by queuing instructions for batch processing rather than committing them to the unit registry on the same business day) does not meet the processing standard and exposes the scheme manager to KiwiSaver Act enforcement risk.

**5.2 Queue management at high-volume periods**

At high-volume periods (e.g., EOFY), the scheme manager must ensure that the switching system and unit registry integration have sufficient capacity to process all submitted instructions within the statutory processing window. The scheme manager cannot cite system capacity constraints as a reason for delayed processing — the obligation is unconditional.

## Part 6 — Records and Audit Trail

Scheme managers must maintain records of all fund switching instructions, including: the date and time of instruction submission, the member identifier, the fund codes involved, the eligibility check outcome, the unit price used, the effective date, the confirmation reference number, and (where a fee was charged or waived) the fee calculation and waiver basis.

Records must be retained for seven years and must be producible within five business days of a written FMA request.
