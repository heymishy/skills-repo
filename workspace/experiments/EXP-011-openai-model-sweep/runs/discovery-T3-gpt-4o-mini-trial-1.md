### Problem Statement

The current system for transaction monitoring related to Anti-Money Laundering (AML) is inadequate, as it lacks real-time alerting capabilities and relies on manual checks that can leave breaches undetected for more than 18 hours. Given the expectations from the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) for near-real-time detection, this poses a significant compliance risk and potential penalties for the organization.

### Opportunity

Implementing a real-time alerting system for transaction threshold breaches can greatly enhance the organization's ability to identify and respond to suspicious activities in a timely manner. This not only ensures compliance with FCA regulations but also strengthens the organization's overall risk management framework.

### Stakeholders Affected

1. **Compliance Officers**: Currently face delays in detection and response time due to manual report checks.
2. **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)**: Responsible for filing Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) and will benefit from quicker identification of breaches.
3. **Risk Team**: Will see reduced exposure to risks associated with unmonitored or undetected threshold breaches.

### Rationale for Immediate Action

With an FCA review scheduled for Q4, it is imperative that the organization demonstrates compliance with near-real-time monitoring requirements. Failure to improve the current system could lead to regulatory scrutiny and reputational damage.

### Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

To address these issues, the following MVP is proposed:

- **Real-time Alerts**: Implement automated alerts that notify the compliance team immediately when a transaction exceeds the £10,000 threshold.
- **Communication Channels**: Alerts will be sent through internal Slack and email to ensure rapid dissemination of critical information.
- **Review Process**: Compliance officers will assess the alerts, deciding whether to clear the alert or escalate it for further investigation.

### Success Metrics

Success will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

1. **Response Time**: Reduction of detection time for threshold breaches from the current average of 18+ hours to under 15 minutes.
2. **Stakeholder Feedback**: Quantitative and qualitative feedback from compliance officers and the MLRO on the effectiveness and usability of the alerting system.
3. **Regulatory Compliance**: Positive assessment from the FCA during the upcoming review, indicating the organization meets near-real-time monitoring expectations.

### Next Steps

1. **System Design**: Collaborate with technical teams to design and implement the alert system.
2. **User Training**: Train compliance staff on the new processes for responding to alerts.
3. **Testing and Validation**: Conduct thorough testing of the system to ensure reliability.
4. **Monitor and Adjust**: Continuously monitor the system performance and make necessary adjustments based on feedback and incident reports.

By addressing the outlined issues effectively, the organization can not only enhance compliance but also strengthen its position in mitigating potential risks associated with money laundering activities.
