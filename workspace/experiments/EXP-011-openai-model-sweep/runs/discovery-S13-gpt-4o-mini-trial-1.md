To successfully build the trans-Tasman payment feature for enterprise retail customers, we should follow a structured approach that addresses the technical, operational, regulatory, and customer experience aspects of the initiative. Here’s a breakdown of the necessary steps and considerations:

### 1. Technical Development
- **Internal Routing Channel**: Develop the proprietary internal routing mechanism that facilitates direct payments between NZ and AU counterparts. The system should leverage existing treasury infrastructure.
- **Integration with Existing Systems**: Ensure that the new payment processing feature integrates seamlessly with current banking platforms, compliance systems, and customer relationship management (CRM) tools.
- **User Interface Design**: Create an intuitive interface for enterprise customers to initiate payments easily, including transaction history and status updates.

### 2. Pricing Strategy
- **Target Pricing Implementation**: Design the payment process to achieve the target pricing of under $5 for transactions up to $10,000. Establish clear guidelines on how payments above this threshold will revert to the SWIFT channel pricing.
- **Fee Transparency**: Clearly communicate all fees to customers upfront to maintain transparency and build trust in the new feature.

### 3. Compliance and Regulatory Considerations
- **AML/CFT Framework**: Extend existing AML/CFT screening protocols to cover the new internal routing channel, including sanctions screening and reporting requirements to the RBNZ.
- **Regulatory Notifications**: Confirm with the regulatory team what specific notifications and approvals are needed prior to launch, including any potential inspections or audits.

### 4. Pilot Launch
- **Cohort Selection**: Identify a small group of enterprise customers to pilot the feature. This group can provide valuable feedback about functionality and user experience.
- **Feedback Mechanism**: Establish a process for collecting and analyzing feedback during the pilot phase to make necessary adjustments before the full rollout.

### 5. Customer Experience Focus
- **Communication Strategy**: Inform customers about the new payment feature, emphasizing benefits such as timeliness, reduced cost, and ease of use. Use email campaigns, webinars, or tutorials to educate customers on the new process.
- **Support and Training**: Provide adequate training for customer service representatives to assist users with the new feature and troubleshoot any issues that arise.

### 6. Monitoring and Evaluation
- **Performance Metrics**: Define key performance indicators (KPIs) to evaluate the success of the payment feature, such as transaction volume, customer satisfaction, feedback scores, and revenue impact.
- **Iterative Improvement**: Use insights from the pilot to iterate on the product before a full launch, focusing on both technology and customer experience enhancements.

### 7. Future Expansion
- **AU-to-NZ Payments**: If the initial launch is successful, begin planning for the expansion of the service to facilitate AU-to-NZ payments. Analyze data from the pilot to support the business case for this next step.

### Timeline
- **6-Month Build**: Establish a timeline for development, testing, and regulatory submissions, ensuring milestones are met along the way.
- **Pilot Phase**: Plan a specific duration for the pilot (e.g., 1-2 months) to gather sufficient data before assessing the feasibility of a broader rollout.

By following this comprehensive approach, we can effectively implement the trans-Tasman payment feature, align it with regulatory requirements, and meet customer expectations, thereby addressing the competitive gap and recapturing lost revenue.
