To develop an automated regulatory reporting pipeline that addresses the challenges faced by your finance operations team, we will outline a comprehensive approach that meets your requirements. This plan aims to streamline the data extraction, reconciliation, and reporting processes while ensuring compliance and auditability.

### Key Components of the Automated Regulatory Reporting Pipeline

1. **Data Extraction Layer:**
   - **API Integration:** Utilize read-only APIs to connect with the core banking, treasury, and card platform systems.
   - **Data Mapping:** Define the specific fields required for regulatory reporting and map them clearly from each source system to ensure consistency.

2. **Data Normalization Layer:**
   - **Normalization Logic Implementation:** Formalize the normalization process to correct rounding differences and timing mismatches directly within the pipeline.
   - **Transformation Rules:** Document and implement the transformation rules consistently across all relevant data fields, ensuring they account for legacy artefacts.

3. **Reconciliation Logic:**
   - **Automated Reconciliation:** Establish an automated reconciliation process that compares the extracted data against predefined benchmarks to ensure accuracy.
   - **Exception Handling:** Develop workflows to flag and manage exceptions for analyst review, minimizing manual intervention.

4. **Audit Trail and Compliance:**
   - **Audit Logging:** Create an immutable audit trail that tracks every transformation, reconciliation step, and data source for compliance purposes.
   - **Traceability:** Ensure that any submitted figures can be traced back to their respective source data, accompanied by a clear explanation of adjustments made.

5. **Review and Approval Workflow:**
   - **User Interface for Analysts:** Design a user-friendly interface where analysts can review the pre-populated return files generated by the pipeline.
   - **Approval Workflow:** Implement a structured approval process within the system, allowing analysts to sign off on the returns before submission.

6. **Submission Process:**
   - **Pre-Submission Checks:** Introduce automated checks to validate the completeness and accuracy of the return files before they are reviewed by analysts.
   - **Submission to Regulator:** Configure the system to facilitate submission via the RBNZ Reporting Portal and FMA's online submission gateway after approval.

7. **Timeliness and Reporting Calendar:**
   - **Schedule Automation:** Set up a scheduling mechanism to ensure the pipeline runs timely each month, allowing ample time for review and submission before the 20th deadline.
   - **Monitoring and Alerts:** Develop real-time monitoring and alerting features to notify the compliance team of any potential delays in the reporting process.

### Implementation Plan

1. **Stakeholder Engagement:** 
   - Work closely with your finance operations team, compliance department, and IT to gather detailed requirements and design the system.

2. **Pilot Testing:**
   - Initially implement the pipeline for a specific reporting cycle to test its efficacy, addressing any issues that arise.

3. **Training and Change Management:**
   - Provide training to analysts on using the new pipeline and address any concerns regarding the automation of their tasks.

4. **Continuous Improvement:**
   - Regularly gather feedback from users to enhance the pipeline's functionality and address any evolving regulatory requirements.

### Expected Benefits

- **Time Savings:** Automating extraction and reconciliation is expected to free up to 40% of analysts’ time, allowing them to focus on higher-value activities.
- **Increased Accuracy:** Reducing manual data handling minimizes the potential for human error and enhances data quality.
- **Enhanced Compliance:** An audit trail ensures transparency and helps the organization remain compliant with RBNZ and FMA regulations.
- **Timely Submissions:** Improved processes will help meet internal and regulatory deadlines, reducing the risk of missed submissions and associated penalties.

Implementing this automated regulatory reporting pipeline will significantly improve the efficiency of your finance operations team while ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
