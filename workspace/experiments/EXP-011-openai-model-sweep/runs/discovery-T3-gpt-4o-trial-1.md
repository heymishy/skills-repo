Based on the information provided, here's a structured plan to address the problem and seize the opportunity for improvement:

### Problem Description
- Lack of real-time transaction monitoring is leading to significant delays in detecting AML threshold breaches.
- Current manual system takes over 18 hours, posing a risk of non-compliance with FCA requirements.

### Affected Parties
- **Compliance Officers:** Delays in transaction reviews increase workload and stress.
- **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer):** Responsible for timely filing of Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs), which could be jeopardized by delays.
- **Risk Team:** Prolonged exposure to and potential repercussions from undetected breaches.

### Urgency
- An FCA review is imminent in Q4, necessitating prompt action to ensure compliance and avoid potential penalties.

### Goals for MVP
- Implement real-time alerts for transactions exceeding the £10,000 threshold.
- Ensure alerts are sent via Slack and email to the compliance officers for immediate attention.
- Facilitating a process where the compliance team can promptly review and decide whether to clear or escalate the transaction.

### Success Criteria
- Reduction in detection time for threshold breaches from over 18 hours to under 15 minutes.
- Reliable, consistent alerting that allows compliance teams to act swiftly.
  
### Steps for Implementation

1. **Technical Requirements Gathering**
   - Determine systems/data integration needs for real-time transaction data.
   - Identify current system limitations and required upgrades or integrations.

2. **System Design & Development**
   - Develop or procure tools capable of real-time transaction data processing.
   - Build an alerting system with integrations to Slack and email for instant notifications.

3. **Testing Phase**
   - Conduct rigorous testing with simulated transaction data to ensure system responsiveness and accuracy.
   - Adjust thresholds/alert definitions to minimize false positives while maintaining sensitivity.

4. **Deployment**
   - Gradual roll-out in controlled environment with a select user group.
   - Gather feedback from compliance officers and MLRO to refine processes.

5. **Training & Documentation**
   - Train compliance team and other stakeholders on new system usage.
   - Develop comprehensive documentation for system protocols and processes.

6. **Monitoring & Continuous Improvement**
   - Implement a feedback loop to continuously evaluate system performance.
   - Regularly update the system based on policy changes or new compliance requirements.

By focusing on these steps and ensuring alignment with stakeholders' needs, you can effectively address the real-time monitoring gap and meet FCA compliance requirements before the scheduled review.
