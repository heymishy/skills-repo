Given the requirements and challenges outlined for the personal lending team, here is a strategic approach to digitizing the personal loan application journey:

### Project Overview
- **Objective**: Create a digital application flow for personal loans that streamlines the customer experience, reduces processing time, and maintains compliance with responsible lending obligations.
- **Target Launch**: Before the end of Q3 to align with the winter lending season.

### Key Components of the Digital Application Flow
1. **User Interface**:
   - Develop a user-friendly interface on both the mobile app and web platform where customers can easily input their loan purpose, amount, and term.
   - Consider incorporating a progress tracker to keep customers informed about the application status.

2. **Data Integration**:
   - Integrate seamlessly with the core banking system to automatically fetch existing transaction histories for customers.
   - Establish connectivity with Centrix or other credit bureau providers to pull credit reports in real time.

3. **Automated Decision Engine**:
   - Develop an automated decision-making algorithm for loans up to $30,000 that utilizes:
     - Historical transaction data.
     - Credit bureau data.
     - Internal risk scorecard.
   - Ensure the algorithm can provide rationale for decisions (e.g., approval or denial reasons) to improve customer understanding.

4. **Manual Review Process**:
   - For applications exceeding $30,000, establish a clear workflow for credit analysts to review applications. This should include a dashboard to manage and track these applications efficiently.

5. **Bias Evaluation and Model Validation**:
   - Conduct an independent validation of the credit decisioning model to assess and mitigate any biases.
   - Develop a transparent framework for monitoring and improving the decision-making model continuously.
   - Document all findings and necessary adjustments to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

6. **Compliance with Responsible Lending Obligations**:
   - Work closely with the legal team to outline how the automated flow can meet the reasonable inquiries obligation.
   - Consider implementing a comprehensive financial questionnaire in the application process to gather necessary information about the customer’s financial situation.

7. **Regulatory Disclosure**:
   - Prepare to disclose findings from the internal review regarding model bias to the FMA to ensure transparency and compliance.
   - Develop an ongoing compliance and risk management strategy to regularly assess and report bias in lending decisions.

### Timeline and Milestones
- **Month 1**: User interface design and prototyping. Start working with legal to ensure compliance.
- **Month 2**: Develop the automated decision engine and integrate necessary data sources. Start the bias validation process.
- **Month 3**: Conduct user testing of the application flow and refine based on feedback. Finalize compliance framework and prepare for launch.
- **End of Month 3 (by end of Q3)**: Launch the digital application flow with a marketing campaign.

### Resources Needed
- **Technology Team**: Software developers, UX/UI designers, data analysts, and systems integrators.
- **Legal and Compliance**: Input from the legal team to ensure the solution is compliant with the Credit Contracts and Consumer Finance Act and FMA's expectations.
- **Credit Analysts**: Process training for manual reviews where applicable, along with support for the decisioning model validation.

### Conclusion
By adhering to this approach, the personal lending team can create an efficient digital application journey that not only meets customer expectations for quick service but also aligns with responsible lending requirements. Timely execution, stakeholder collaboration, and a focus on compliance will be crucial to the project's success.
