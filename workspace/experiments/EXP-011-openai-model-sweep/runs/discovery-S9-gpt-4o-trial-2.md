To implement an online fund switching feature effectively and ensure that it is ready by the 31 March deadline, here's a general plan and key considerations you need to address:

### Project Plan:

1. **Project Kick-off and Planning:**
   - Define project scope and objectives clearly.
   - Assemble a project team, including representatives from IT, operations, compliance, finance, and member services.
   - Establish a project timeline, phase deadlines, and key milestones, including a March 31 go-live date.

2. **Requirements Gathering:**
   - Engage stakeholders (including members for feedback on convenience and usability) to understand all necessary functionality.
   - Identify and document detailed requirements for the feature, including online UI/UX design, backend processes, data integration, security, and notification systems.

3. **Technology Infrastructure:**
   - Select or validate technology platforms for building the online fund switch feature — consider existing portal capabilities, integration with the unit registry, and transaction processing systems.
   - Partner with IT teams to ensure server capacity, cybersecurity measures, and redundancy protocols are in place for handling peak loads.

4. **Implementation:**
   - Develop the online interface that allows members to log in, view, and switch funds.
   - Implement backend processing to commit fund switches to the unit registry in real-time or at scheduled intervals throughout the business day.
   - Integrate communication channels to send automated confirmations and processing estimate notifications.

5. **Compliance and Notifications:**
   - Finalize member notification plans and ensure compliance with Financial Markets Authority guidelines.
   - Draft and approve communication to inform members about the changes, including fund-switching process updates and the new switching fee policy.

6. **Testing and Quality Assurance:**
   - Conduct thorough testing cycles, including functional, integration, regression, security, and user acceptance testing (UAT).
   - Test the handling of edge cases where manual intervention may be required, ensuring those processes are defined and operationally feasible.

7. **Training and Support:**
   - Provide training for member services and operations teams on the new system and processes, including how to manage exceptions.
   - Establish dedicated support channels to quickly address member inquiries and issues related to the new feature.

8. **Launch and Go-live:**
   - Execute a phased rollout if feasible, starting with a pilot group of members to gather real-time feedback and make adjustments.
   - Ensure there is additional customer support available around the launch period, particularly close to the March 31 deadline.

9. **Post-launch Monitoring and Adjustments:**
   - Monitor system performance, member uptake, satisfaction levels, and operational impacts post-launch.
   - Be ready to make rapid adjustments to address any issues, especially those affecting real-time switch processing.

### Key Considerations:

- **Regulatory Compliance:** Ensure all processes comply with local regulations, and liaise with regulatory bodies to preempt any potential issues.
  
- **Data Security:** Protect member information and ensure secure transactions throughout the switch process, adhering to best practices in data protection.

- **Member Communication:** Address expectations around the March 31 deadline proactively in communications, emphasizing the requirement for requests to be submitted by 3pm.

- **Fee Transparency:** Clearly communicate your policy on the $15 switching fee, including conditions under which it applies, to mitigate member dissatisfaction.

- **Fallback Processes:** Solidify the processes and resource allocation for handling edge cases via paper-based methods until online capabilities can be expanded to cover additional scenarios. 

By following this approach, your team can ensure a successful implementation of the fund switching feature, thus improving member satisfaction and reducing churn.
