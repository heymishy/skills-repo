Understood, you're proposing a note-taking app for meeting notes and decisions. Given your firm's context—regulated financial services, 500+ employees—let's proceed with the /discovery pipeline.

**Initial Assessment (Discovery Stage 1):**

1. **Problem Definition**  
   Teams lack a centralized, compliant space to capture and access meeting notes and decisions.

2. **Objectives**  
   * Improve record-keeping of meetings/decisions.  
   * Enable knowledge sharing and searchability.  
   * Ensure compliance with industry data security and retention rules.

3. **Key Constraints (Based on product/constraints.md & your context):**  
   - Data residency and encryption requirements.  
   - Auditability for all content and edits.  
   - User authentication via SSO (likely via your existing identity provider).  
   - Integration with existing productivity tools (e.g., Outlook, Teams, SharePoint).  
   - Access permissions aligned with internal org roles.  
   - No third-party SaaS storing sensitive data outside approved regions.

**Questions Before Proceeding (To Validate Fit and Priorities):**  
- What are current pain points with existing tools (e.g., OneNote, SharePoint, Confluence)?  
- What level/type of integration is needed with meeting platforms (e.g., Microsoft Teams, Zoom)?  
- Who are the target users—frontline, back office, management?  
- Are there established compliance workflows for records retention, and how should notes flow into them?  
- Is there a preference for a web app, desktop app, or addition to existing internal portals?

**Next Steps:**  
- Gather feedback from 2–3 representative teams on specific note-taking requirements and compliance gaps.  
- Map existing workflows/tools and identify integration touchpoints.  
- Review regulatory obligations (GDPR, FCA, MiFID II, SOX, etc.) related to meeting documentation.

**Summary:**  
Building a centralized note-taking app is viable, but must be designed for compliance, security, and seamless integration with your existing stack.
