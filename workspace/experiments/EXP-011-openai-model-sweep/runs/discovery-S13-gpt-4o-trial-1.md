You've outlined a strategic plan to create an internal payment feature that leverages existing relationships and infrastructure to provide faster and cheaper transfer options for your customers. Here's a detailed breakdown of how you might approach this:

### Objective
- Develop a fast, cost-effective trans-Tasman payment solution for NZ enterprise retail customers.
- Reduce the competitive gap from third-party services like Wise, capturing lost revenue from international payments.

### Key Features
- **Proprietary Internal Routing Channel**: Leverage the relationship with your Australian counterpart to facilitate quick settlements via treasury books.
- **Fast Settlement**: Aim for a customer-facing settlement experience within 2 hours.
- **Competitive Pricing**: Transactions priced under $5 for amounts up to $10,000.

### Technical and Operational Considerations
- **Channel Infrastructure**: Utilize shared group infrastructure to ensure seamless processing at both NZ and AU ends.
- **End-of-Day Treasury Netting**: Implement a system to settle net positions between counterparties at the end of each business day.
- **AML/CFT Compliance**: Extend existing SWIFT-based AML/CFT and sanctions screening processes to the new payment channel.
- **Scalability**: Initially focus on NZ-to-AU payments, with potential future expansion to include AU-to-NZ payments.

### Regulatory Considerations
- **Ensure Compliance**: Work with your regulatory team to handle necessary notifications and adhere to local and international payment regulations.
- **Risk Management**: Regular audits and risk assessments to ensure ongoing compliance and operational integrity.

### Implementation Timeline
- **Development**: A 6-month build period leveraging current IT and treasury infrastructure.
- **Pilot Program**: Conduct a pilot with a select group of customers to gather feedback and ensure system robustness.
- **Full Rollout**: Based on pilot results, proceed with wider implementation.

### Customer Experience
- **User Interface**: Simplified process for initiating transfers with real-time status updates.
- **Customer Support**: Dedicated support channels for handling inquiries and troubleshooting issues specific to the new payment feature.

### Pricing Strategy
- **Tiered Pricing Model**: Sub-$5 fees for payments under $10,000, keeping the SWIFT pricing model for amounts above this threshold.
- **Incentives**: Consider introductory offers or loyalty points to encourage early adoption and customer engagement.

### Marketing and Communication
- **Awareness Campaigns**: Inform customers about the new feature, its benefits, and ease of use compared to existing alternatives.
- **Feedback Loops**: Establish channels for customers to provide feedback and suggestions post-launch.

### Risk and Mitigation
- **System Reliability**: Ensure robust IT systems to handle potential increases in transaction volume.
- **Customer Transition**: Provide educational materials (e.g., guides, webinars) to help customers transition from existing services to the new offering smoothly.
  
By executing on these plans, you'll be able to reclaim revenue lost to third parties, enhance customer satisfaction with faster service, and strengthen your competitive position in the market.
