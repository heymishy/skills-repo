### Problem Statement

We need to enhance our transaction monitoring capabilities to ensure compliance with the FCA's expectations for near-real-time detection of Anti-Money Laundering (AML) threshold breaches. Our current manual reporting system is insufficient and poses a risk to our organization, as demonstrated by two near-misses in the last 90 days.

### Stakeholders Affected

1. **Compliance Officers**: Currently receive daily reports, leading to delayed responses to potential AML breaches.
2. **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)**: Responsible for filing Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs); delayed detection could affect timely reporting and compliance.
3. **Risk Team**: Exposed to operational risks due to undetected breaches.

### Urgency of the Initiative

The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) review scheduled for Q4 requires demonstrable progress in our transaction monitoring capabilities. Delays in implementing a solution could lead to regulatory repercussions and damage to our reputation.

### Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

- Develop a real-time alerting system to notify the compliance team of transactions that breach the £10,000 threshold.
- Integrate alerts with internal communication tools (Slack) and email for immediate visibility.
- Establish a protocol for compliance officers to review alerts and either clear or escalate them as necessary.

### Success Metrics

- **Response Time**: Reduce detection and response time from over 18 hours to under 15 minutes.
- **Alert Accuracy**: Aim for a low false positive rate, ensuring alerts are relevant and actionable.
- **Compliance Readiness**: Demonstrate system effectiveness and timeliness during the upcoming FCA review.

### Next Steps

1. **Requirements Gathering**: Collaborate with compliance officers and the risk team to gather detailed requirements for the alerting system.
2. **System Design**: Develop a technical design for the real-time alert system, considering data sources, integration points, and user interface requirements.
3. **Development**: Build the system and test it in a controlled environment to ensure reliability and accuracy.
4. **Training and Rollout**: Provide training to compliance officers on the new system, and prepare for a phased rollout.
5. **Monitoring and Iteration**: Continuously monitor the system's performance and gather feedback for ongoing improvements.

By addressing this problem promptly, we can ensure that our organization meets regulatory requirements and mitigates risks associated with AML compliance.
