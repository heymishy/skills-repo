To successfully implement the online fund switching feature, we should focus on the following key aspects:

1. **Technical Implementation:**
   - Develop the online fund switching functionality within the member portal. Ensure that the interface is user-friendly, allowing members to easily select their target fund and confirm their switch.
   - Integrate with the unit registry system to commit switch instructions on the same business day. Ensure this process is automated and reliable.
   - Implement backend capabilities to deliver real-time switch confirmation and estimated processing dates to the members.
   - Develop a robust testing plan to ensure the feature works flawlessly under various scenarios.

2. **Compliance and Communication:**
   - Coordinate closely with the compliance team to finalize notifications for the Financial Markets Authority, ensuring all regulatory requirements are met.
   - Develop a communication plan to inform members well in advance about the new online switching feature, including details about the $15 fee for additional switches beyond the second one per year.

3. **Project Management:**
   - Set milestones and deadlines to ensure the project is on track for the 31 March delivery date. Consider using agile methodology to allow flexibility and iterative progress.
   - Allocate resources effectively, ensuring that critical areas like IT development, compliance, operations, and customer service are appropriately staffed.

4. **Operational Considerations:**
   - Plan for edge cases and ensure that operations are prepared to handle situations involving new members, those on contribution holidays, or those with hardship applications manually.
   - Define clear procedures for these manual processes, ensuring that there's minimal impact on the automated system's efficiency.

5. **User Experience:**
   - Focus on creating a seamless experience for the user, with clear instructions and intuitive navigation throughout the fund switching process.
   - Consider the placement of help resources or FAQs to assist members in understanding their options and the switching process.

6. **Risk Management:**
   - Identify potential risks such as technical failures, non-compliance, or delays in deployment, and develop mitigation strategies for each.
   - Plan for post-launch monitoring and rapid response capability to address any unforeseen issues.

Launching the online fund switching feature effectively and on time is critical to maintaining member trust and competitiveness. Leveraging clear project planning, compliance adherence, and member communication will be instrumental in achieving a successful rollout by the 31 March deadline.
