# Discovery: Consent Management API — Open Banking Programme

## 1. Initiative Summary

Build a consent management API enabling accredited third parties to access customer financial data (transactions, balances, credit card summaries, and enriched insights) with granular, time-limited, revocable customer consent, validated against an external accreditation authority on every call. Target: 10 accredited partners live within 6 months, enriched insights in launch scope.

---

## 2. What's Well-Defined (Strengths of the Brief)

- **Granular consent model** — per data type, per third party, time-limited, revocable. This is the right foundation.
- **Continuous accreditation validation** — checking on every API call rather than only at onboarding is best practice and ahead of many implementations.
- **Clear data taxonomy** — four data types with defined boundaries (12-month transaction window, etc.).
- **Privacy engagement already underway** — privacy team has reviewed the consent model.

---

## 3. Critical Gaps & Risks

### 🔴 R1 — Enriched insights are derived/inferred data, not raw data. This changes the privacy analysis.
Spending categorisation, **estimated income**, and a **90-day projected balance** are inferences generated by your analytics engine. This raises issues raw data doesn't:

- **Accuracy obligations.** Estimated income and projected balances are predictions. If a third party (e.g. a lender or comparison service) uses an inaccurate income estimate in a credit-relevant decision, who bears responsibility? Privacy law accuracy obligations (e.g. APP 10-style) apply to inferred personal information you disclose.
- **Consent comprehension.** Customers must understand they're consenting to share *inferences about them*, not just their records. "Estimated monthly income" is materially more sensitive than a transaction list — it's a profile.
- **Was this specifically reviewed?** The brief says privacy reviewed "the overall consent model." Confirm in writing that the review explicitly covered the enriched insights tier as derived data, not just the consent mechanics.
- **Regulatory regime fit.** If this operates under a formal regime (e.g. CDR in Australia, Open Banking in the UK), derived data and value-added insights may sit under different rules than mandated product data — possibly outside the regime entirely, requiring a separate contractual/consent framework.

**Recommendation:** Treat enriched insights as a separately gated scope item with its own privacy impact assessment. Consider phasing it (see §6).

### 🔴 R2 — Revocation deletion is asserted, not designed.
"On revocation, the third party must delete all data" is a contractual obligation, not a mechanism. Open questions:

- How is revocation **communicated** to the third party (webhook, polling, batch)? What SLA for deletion?
- How is deletion **verified or attested**? Audit rights? Deletion certificates?
- Does the obligation extend to **data the third party derived** from your data, and to data passed to *their* downstream providers (cloud, analytics, fourth parties)?
- Some regimes permit **de-identification as an alternative to deletion**, sometimes at the customer's election. Which applies here?

### 🔴 R3 — Loss of accreditation handles *future* access only.
"Blocked from new data access immediately" — correct, but incomplete:
- What happens to data **already held** by a de-accredited party? Deletion trigger? Customer notification?
- Are affected customers told their active consents have been suspended/terminated, and is this visible in the portal?

### 🟠 R4 — Joint accounts and multi-party authorisation are unaddressed.
This is one of the hardest problems in every open banking programme. Does one joint holder's consent suffice, or do all holders approve? Can one holder veto or revoke? This affects data model, consent flows, and portal UX, and cannot be retrofitted easily. **Decision needed before API design.**

### 🟠 R5 — Consent lifecycle edges are undefined.
- **Expiry:** what happens at the 12-month mark — silent lapse, customer notification, re-authorisation flow? Does expiry trigger the same third-party deletion obligation as revocation?
- **Amendment:** can a customer narrow a consent (remove one data type) without revoke-and-recreate?
- **Account-level granularity:** consent is per data type and per third party — but is it per *account*? A customer with three accounts may want to share only one.
- **Account closure, deceased customers, hardship/vulnerability flags:** what happens to active consents?

### 🟠 R6 — Availability dependency on the accreditation authority.
Per-call validation means the accreditation authority's availability/latency is now in your critical path. You need a defined caching strategy (e.g. short-TTL cached status with fail-closed or fail-open policy — **fail-closed recommended**, with a documented stance) and an SLA conversation with the authority.

### 🟡 R7 — Customer transparency beyond grant/revoke.
Meaningful control implies customers can see: active consents, what data types, which party, expiry date, and ideally an **access log** ("Partner X retrieved your transactions on these dates"). Is the consent dashboard in scope for launch?

### 🟡 R8 — Timeline risk.
Six months for: consent engine, portal consent UX, third-party onboarding, accreditation integration, security model (presumably FAPI-grade OAuth2/mTLS), revocation mechanics, plus enriched insights productisation, plus 10 partner integrations. The enriched insights tier is the most novel, least regulated, and most privacy-sensitive component — it is the natural candidate to de-risk the date.

## 4. Key Assumptions to Validate

| # | Assumption | Validate with |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| A1 | The programme operates under a defined regulatory regime, and enriched (derived) data is permissible within it | Legal / regulatory affairs |
| A2 | Privacy review covered derived-data disclosure specifically, not just consent mechanics | Privacy team (get it in writing) |
| A3 | The accreditation authority offers a real-time status API with an SLA suitable for per-call checks | Accreditation authority |
| A4 | The analytics engine's insights are accurate enough to disclose externally, with known error characteristics | Analytics/data science team |
| A5 | Third parties will accept contractual deletion-on-revocation terms incl. derived data and audit rights | Legal / partner management |
| A6 | Member portal can host consent grant/manage/revoke flows in the timeline | Portal product team |

## 5. Prioritised Open Questions

**Must answer before design begins:**
1. Which regulatory regime governs this, and does it cover derived/insight data? (R1)
2. Joint account consent model — one holder or all? (R4)
3. Deletion vs de-identification on revocation, and the enforcement/attestation mechanism? (R2)
4. Is consent granular per account, or only per data type + third party? (R5)

**Must answer before build completes:**
5. Fail-open vs fail-closed when the accreditation authority is unreachable? (R6)
6. Full consent lifecycle state machine — expiry, amendment, suspension, closure, death/hardship? (R5)
7. Data held by de-accredited parties — deletion and customer notification? (R3)
8. Consent dashboard and access log — launch scope or fast-follow? (R7)
9. Liability and disclaimer position on income estimates and balance projections used by third parties? (R1)

**Should answer for launch readiness:**
10. Success metrics — what does "meaningful control" measure as? (consent completion rates, revocation friction, complaint volumes, partner adoption)
11. Customer correction flows — if a customer disputes an income estimate, what happens to data already shared?

---

## 6. Scope Recommendation

**Phase 1 (months 0–6):** Raw data tiers — transactions, balances, credit card summaries — with full consent lifecycle, revocation mechanics, accreditation validation, and consent dashboard. Launch with the 10-partner cohort.

**Phase 2 (months 6–9):** Enriched insights tier, gated on:
- A dedicated PIA covering derived-data disclosure
- Confirmed regulatory treatment of derived data
- Accuracy benchmarking and disclosure language for income/projection estimates
- Contractual terms covering third-party use limitations on inferences
