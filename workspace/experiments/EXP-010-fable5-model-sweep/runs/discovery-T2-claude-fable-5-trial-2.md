# Discovery Pipeline — Step 1: Framing

**Captured problem statement:**
> Improve the onboarding experience for new business customers.

This is a solid starting point, but it's currently a *solution-shaped goal* ("improve onboarding") rather than a defined problem. Before generating hypotheses, research plans, or opportunity sizing, I need to narrow the frame. A few targeted questions:

## Clarifying questions

**1. Context — what's the product/service?**
What are customers onboarding *into*? (e.g., SaaS platform, banking product, marketplace, internal tool)

**2. Who counts as a "business customer"?**
SMBs self-serving, mid-market with sales-assist, enterprise with implementation teams — or a mix? The onboarding problems differ radically across these.

**3. What does "onboarding" span today?**
Where does it start (signup? contract signed?) and end (first login? first value moment? 90-day activation?)

**4. Why now — what's the signal?**
What evidence suggests onboarding is a problem? e.g.:
- Drop-off / activation metrics
- Support ticket volume
- Churn in the first 90 days
- Sales/CS anecdotes
- Competitive pressure

**5. What does "improved" mean?**
What metric would tell you this worked? (time-to-first-value, activation rate, NPS, support deflection, expansion revenue?)

**6. Constraints**
Timeline, team capacity, parts of onboarding that are off-limits (e.g., compliance/KYC steps you can't change)?
