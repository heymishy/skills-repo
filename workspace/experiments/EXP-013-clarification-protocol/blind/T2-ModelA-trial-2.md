# Discovery Pipeline — Stage 1: Problem Framing

Thanks — that's a solid starting point, but it's currently a **solution-space-adjacent goal**, not yet a framed problem. Before I generate hypotheses, research plans, or opportunity maps, I need enough context to avoid building on assumptions.

**Answer what you can below. Skip anything unknown — gaps are useful signals too.**

---

## 1. Context & Trigger
- What product or service are these business customers onboarding into? (e.g., SaaS platform, banking, logistics, telecom)
- **Why now?** What triggered this — churn data, support volume, a stakeholder mandate, competitive pressure, anecdotes?

## 2. Evidence of the Problem
- What signals suggest onboarding is broken? (e.g., activation rate, time-to-first-value, drop-off points, NPS/CSAT, support tickets)
- Do you have any existing research, funnel data, or customer feedback I should treat as inputs?

## 3. Scope & Definitions
- What counts as "onboarding" here — from signup to first login? To first value moment? First 90 days?
- Who is the "new business customer"? (SMB vs. enterprise, self-serve vs. sales-assisted, specific segments?)

## 4. Success & Constraints
- If this discovery succeeds, what changes? What metric or decision does it inform?
- Any constraints: timeline, access to customers, teams involved, things already ruled in/out?

---

**Option A — Answer the questions** (even partially), and I'll produce a scoped problem statement, riskiest assumptions, and a discovery plan.

**Option B — Say "proceed with assumptions"**, and I'll draft a discovery brief for a *generic B2B SaaS onboarding* scenario with all assumptions explicitly flagged for you to correct.
