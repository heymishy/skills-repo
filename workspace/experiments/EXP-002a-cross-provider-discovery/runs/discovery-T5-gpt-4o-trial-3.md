To build a note-taking app tailored for a regulated financial services firm with over 500 employees, we need to consider several aspects based on the product context and constraints. Here's how we can approach the discovery phase:

### Key Considerations:

1. **Regulatory Compliance:**
   - Ensure the app complies with financial regulations such as GDPR, CCPA, or industry-specific requirements like SEC rules.
   - Implement strict data encryption, both at rest and in transit, to protect sensitive information.

2. **Data Security and Privacy:**
   - Provide secure authentication mechanisms (e.g., SSO with two-factor authentication).
   - Incorporate user role management to control access to notes and decision logs.

3. **Integration with Existing Systems:**
   - Enable integration with commonly used tools and platforms (e.g., Microsoft Office 365, Google Workspace, or any bespoke enterprise systems).
   - Facilitate data import/export capabilities to ensure seamless information flow.

4. **Collaboration Features:**
   - Support real-time collaborative editing.
   - Provide version control and audit trails to track changes and who made them.

5. **Search and Organization:**
   - Implement robust search functionality to quickly find past notes and decisions.
   - Allow users to categorize and tag notes for better organization.

6. **User Experience:**
   - Design a user-friendly interface that requires minimum training.
   - Ensure the app is accessible across devices (desktop, tablet, mobile).

7. **Scalability and Performance:**
   - Build an architecture capable of scaling with the organization.
   - Optimize performance for quick access to notes and minimal downtime.

8. **Customization:**
   - Allow teams to customize templates for different types of meetings.
   - Enable personalized dashboards for different user roles.

### Discovery Phase Steps:

1. **Stakeholder Interviews:**
   - Conduct interviews with potential users across various departments to gather requirements.
   - Understand specific pain points with current note-taking processes.

2. **Competitive Analysis:**
   - Research existing note-taking solutions and their shortcomings specific to financial services.

3. **Define Use Cases:**
   - Identify primary use cases like meeting notes capture, decision logging, and action items tracking.

4. **Prototype Development:**
   - Create low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes to visualize the app’s layout and workflow.
   - Gather feedback from a small group of users to refine the design.

5. **Technology Assessment:**
   - Evaluate technology stacks that align with security and compliance needs.
   - Determine if cloud-based or on-premise deployment is more suitable.

6. **Risk Assessment:**
   - Identify potential risks, including data breaches or integration challenges, and outline mitigation strategies.
