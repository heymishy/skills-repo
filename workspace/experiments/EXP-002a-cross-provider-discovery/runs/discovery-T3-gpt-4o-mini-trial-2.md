### Problem Statement
The current lack of real-time alerting for transaction monitoring alerts related to Anti-Money Laundering (AML) threshold breaches poses significant risks to compliance and regulatory obligations. The existing system only flags transactions over £10,000 in cash equivalents, and compliance officers are required to manually review reports each morning. This process has resulted in multiple near misses in detecting breaches, which the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) expects to be managed with near-real-time capabilities.

### Opportunity
By implementing a real-time alerting system for transaction breaches, we can significantly enhance our compliance operations. This would not only streamline the detection process but also align our capabilities with regulatory expectations (the FCA's guidelines), thus reducing the risk of exposure and non-compliance.

### Stakeholders Affected
- **Compliance Officers:** Experience delays in detection and response, which may hinder their ability to act swiftly on breaches.
- **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer):** Faces potential regulatory scrutiny and risks being unable to fulfill their duty to report suspicious activity promptly.
- **Risk Team:** Exposed to increased risk due to undetected breaches, which could result in operational and reputational damage.

### Timeline Consideration
With an FCA review scheduled for Q4, we must act quickly to implement real-time monitoring capabilities to demonstrate our commitment to compliance and risk management.

### Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
To address the problem, we propose developing a real-time alerting system that includes the following features:
1. **Transaction Monitoring:** Automatically flag any transaction that breaches the £10,000 threshold.
2. **Real-Time Alerts:** Notify the compliance team through internal Slack messages and emails immediately upon detection of breaches.
3. **Review and Escalation Workflow:** Allow compliance officers to quickly review flagged transactions, providing options to clear or escalate as necessary.

### Success Metrics
Success will be defined by achieving the following outcomes:
- Reduce the detection and response time for threshold breaches from 18+ hours to under 15 minutes.
- Ensure a significant decrease in near misses and improve overall compliance posture.
- Enhance the confidence of the compliance team, MLRO, and risk management in handling AML obligations.

### Next Steps
1. **Requirement Gathering:** Collaborate with compliance, IT, and risk teams to define the detailed requirements for the real-time alerting system.
2. **Solution Design:** Develop a technical solution that integrates with existing transaction monitoring systems.
3. **Implementation:** Build and test the MVP to ensure it meets compliance requirements and integrates smoothly with internal notification systems.
4. **Training & Rollout:** Prepare training materials for the compliance team on using the new system effectively, followed by phased deployment to ensure smooth adoption. 

By taking these steps, we can significantly enhance our monitoring capabilities and align our operations with regulatory expectations.
