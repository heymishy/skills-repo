**Problem Statement:**

Our current transaction monitoring system poses significant compliance risks as it relies on manual report checks by compliance officers, leading to delayed detection of AML threshold breaches (transactions over £10,000 in cash equivalents). This approach has resulted in two notable near-misses in the past 90 days, where breaches remained undetected for over 18 hours. With an upcoming review from the FCA in Q4, we urgently need to enhance our monitoring capabilities to meet regulatory expectations and mitigate risk exposure.

**Opportunity Identification:**

1. **Real-Time Alert Capability:** Implement a system that notifies compliance personnel immediately upon detection of any transactions exceeding the £10,000 threshold.
  
2. **Integration with Communication Tools:** Utilize existing internal communication platforms (e.g., Slack and email) for alerts, facilitating prompt action by compliance officers.

3. **Streamlined Review Process:** Develop a straightforward process for compliance officers to review, address, and escalate flagged transactions rapidly. 

4. **Improved Compliance Posture:** By improving detection speed, we can enhance our firm's compliance standing, decrease the likelihood of regulatory penalties, and protect the organization from reputational risk associated with AML breaches.

**Stakeholder Consideration:**

- **Compliance Officers:** Will benefit from the automation of alerts, allowing more timely decision-making.
- **Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO):** Will have a more reliable system for filing suspicious activity reports (SARs) and managing risks associated with delayed detection.
- **Risk Team:** Reduced exposure to undiscovered breaches, contributing to a more robust risk management framework.

**MVP Goals:**

- Implement a real-time alert system for transactions that exceed £10,000.
- Set up alerts through internal channels like Slack and email for immediate visibility.
- Ensure compliance officers can efficiently review alerts, take necessary actions, and manage escalation.

**Success Metrics:**

- Shift average detection time of threshold breaches from over 18 hours to under 15 minutes.
- Achieve a measurable decrease in the volume of undetected transactions.
- Improve the compliance team’s operational efficiency and confidence in the detection process.

**Next Steps:**

1. **Technology Assessment:** Evaluate current systems to determine the best approach for real-time alerting capabilities.
2. **System Development:** Collaborate with IT/tech teams to develop and test the alerting mechanism.
3. **Training & Implementation:** Provide training for compliance officers on the new process and conduct a phased rollout.
4. **Feedback Loop:** Establish a mechanism for continuous feedback and improvement based on usage and outcomes post-implementation.

By addressing this critical compliance need with thoughtful technology adoption and process improvement, we can effectively manage AML risks and align with regulatory expectations.
