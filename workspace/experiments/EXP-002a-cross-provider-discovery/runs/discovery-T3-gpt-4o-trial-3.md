**Problem/Opportunity Exploration:**

The current transaction monitoring system is inadequate for meeting the regulatory requirements set by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), which demands near-real-time detection of AML (Anti-Money Laundering) threshold breaches. At present, the system’s inability to provide timely alerts results in lagged response times, putting the organization at risk of regulatory non-compliance and potential financial and reputational damage.

**Key Problems Identified:**

1. **Detection Lag:** Transactions over £10,000 are flagged but only reviewed manually, with significant delays (18+ hours) from occurrence to detection.
2. **Compliance Risks:** The need for timely SARs (Suspicious Activity Reports) filings is critical, as the FCA review is upcoming, and current processes may be deemed insufficient. 
3. **Near-Misses:** Recent incidents where threshold breaches were detected late highlight the urgency for improvement to avoid future compliance failures.

**Affected Parties:**

- **Compliance Officers:** They endure manual, time-consuming processes, potentially leading to oversight and delayed responses.
- **MLRO:** Faces direct responsibility for regulatory breaches due to delayed detection and reporting.
- **Risk Team:** Increased exposure to risks associated with undetected breaches, affecting risk management and mitigation efforts.

**Why Address Now:**

The urgency is driven by the forthcoming FCA review in Q4, necessitating immediate enhancements to assure regulators of the organization’s commitment to AML compliance. Failure to upgrade the system could result in penalties or mandatory corrective action, affecting business operations.

**Minimal Viable Product (MVP) Proposal:**

A real-time alert system will be developed to monitor transactions exceeding the £10,000 threshold and immediately notify the compliance team. Notifications will be delivered through internal communication channels, such as Slack, and supplemented by email alerts. This streamlined alert mechanism allows compliance officers to swiftly review and action breaches, ensuring adherence to compliance standards.

**MVP Outcomes:**

- **Real-Time Alerting:** Compliance officers are alerted within 15 minutes of a breach.
- **Response Efficiency:** Enables rapid evaluation, processing, and escalation of suspicious transactions.
- **Enhanced Compliance:** Aligns monitoring practices with FCA expectations, helping to avoid regulatory scrutiny.

**Defining Success:**

The implementation will be deemed successful when detection time is reduced from over 18 hours to under 15 minutes, ensuring timely regulatory responses and reducing AML compliance risk. This proactive approach will enhance organizational readiness and mitigate the chance of future near-misses, leading to improved overall compliance efficacy.
