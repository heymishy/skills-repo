**Problem Statement:**

The current transaction monitoring system for detecting Anti-Money Laundering (AML) threshold breaches is inadequate, as it relies on manual daily reporting, leading to significant delays in detection and response. The absence of real-time alerting has resulted in compliance risks, highlighted by two near-misses in the past 90 days where threshold breaches were not identified for over 18 hours. With an upcoming FCA review in Q4, the organization requires an immediate solution to ensure compliance with regulatory expectations for near-real-time detection of suspicious transactions.

**Opportunity:** 

Implementing a real-time alerting system for transactions that breach the £10,000 threshold will enhance compliance efforts, reduce detection time, and minimize potential exposure risks. This presents an opportunity to improve operational efficiency, strengthen compliance measures, and build trust with regulators and stakeholders. 

---

**Key Stakeholders Affected:**

1. **Compliance Officers:** 
   - Currently face delays in detecting breaches, affecting their workflow and compliance obligations.

2. **Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO):** 
   - Responsible for escalating breaches and filing Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs), thus impacted by the lag in detection.

3. **Risk Team:** 
   - Exposed to regulatory and financial risks due to undetected breaches, which may affect the organization's reputation and operational integrity. 

---

**Rationale for Immediate Implementation:**

With the FCA review slated for Q4, it is crucial to demonstrate robust transaction monitoring capabilities that align with regulatory requirements. The urgency is amplified by previous incidents where late detection could have led to serious compliance repercussions.

---

**Minimum Viable Product (MVP):**

1. **Real-time Alerts:**
   - Develop a system that triggers alerts for compliance officers when transactions exceed the £10,000 threshold.

2. **Alert Channels:**
   - Implement notifications via internal Slack and email to ensure immediate awareness and action.

3. **Review and Escalation Process:**
   - Establish a protocol for compliance officers to review flagged transactions, clear them if valid, or escalate them for further investigation.

---

**Success Metrics:**

- **Detection Time Improvement:** 
   Target a reduction in detection time of threshold breaches from over 18 hours to under 15 minutes.

- **Incident Rate Reduction:** 
   Minimize the incidence of near misses and undetected breaches to ensure compliance with FCA expectations and maintain organizational integrity.

- **Stakeholder Feedback:** 
   Gather feedback from compliance officers and the MLRO on the usability and effectiveness of the alerting system, ensuring it meets their operational needs.

---

By addressing these factors, we can significantly enhance our AML transaction monitoring capabilities while ensuring compliance with regulatory standards and minimizing risks to the organization.
