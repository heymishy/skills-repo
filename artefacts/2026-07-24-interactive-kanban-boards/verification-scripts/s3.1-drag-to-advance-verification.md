## Setup

**Before you start:**
1. Open a kanban board with a ready card, a not-ready card, and at least two columns.

**Reset between scenarios:** Reload the board between scenarios.

---

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Dragging a ready card to its next column advances it

**Covers:** AC1

**Steps:**
1. Click and hold the ready card.
2. Drag it to its next column (the one immediately after its current stage) and release.

**Expected outcome:** The card stays in the new column after the drop, and reloading the page confirms it's really moved (not just a visual trick).

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 2: Dragging to the wrong column doesn't do anything

**Covers:** AC2

**Steps:**
1. Drag the ready card to a column that ISN'T its correct next stage (e.g. skip two columns ahead).

**Expected outcome:** The card snaps back to where it started — nothing actually changes.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 3: Dragging a not-ready card doesn't silently do nothing

**Covers:** AC3

**Steps:**
1. Try to drag the not-ready card to its next column.

**Expected outcome:** Either you can't pick it up at all, or it snaps back with the same "not ready" explanation from earlier (S1.2) — you're not left confused about what happened.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 4: A real failure during drag shows the real reason

**Covers:** AC4

**Steps:**
1. Arrange (with engineering help) for a card to have an artefact that will fail validation.
2. Drag that card to its next column.

**Expected outcome:** The card snaps back, and you see the actual reason it failed — not a generic error.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 5: You can still advance a card without dragging

**Covers:** AC5

**Steps:**
1. Use the click-based "Advance" button (from the earlier story) on a ready card instead of dragging.

**Expected outcome:** It works exactly the same as before — drag-and-drop hasn't replaced or broken the click option.

**Pass / Fail:** ___
