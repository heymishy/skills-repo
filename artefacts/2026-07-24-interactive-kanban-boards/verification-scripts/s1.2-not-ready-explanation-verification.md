## Setup

**Before you start:**
1. Open a kanban board with at least one card whose session is mid-conversation (not ready to advance) and one ready card.

**Reset between scenarios:** Reload the board page between scenarios.

---

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: A not-ready card explains why, in plain language

**Covers:** AC1

**Steps:**
1. Find the card whose session is mid-conversation.
2. Look at the card.

**Expected outcome:** The card shows a clearly-worded note (not just a colour change) saying something like "still in progress" or naming the current stage — not a cryptic code or blank space.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 2: Hovering or tabbing to the card shows more detail

**Covers:** AC2

**Steps:**
1. Hover your mouse over the not-ready card, or press Tab until it's focused (keyboard).

**Expected outcome:** A more detailed explanation appears (tooltip or expanded text) without needing to click through to another page.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 3: A ready card looks clearly different — no "not ready" warning

**Covers:** AC3

**Steps:**
1. Find a ready card.
2. Compare it to the not-ready card from Scenario 1.

**Expected outcome:** The ready card shows no "not ready" warning, and its Advance action is clearly visible and clickable.

**Pass / Fail:** ___

---

### Scenario 4: A real error looks different from "not ready yet"

**Covers:** AC4

**Steps:**
1. Arrange (with engineering help) for a card to hit a real validation failure when advanced.
2. Compare its appearance to the not-ready card from Scenario 1.

**Expected outcome:** The two look and read differently — a real error should not look like a routine "still in progress" state.

**Pass / Fail:** ___
