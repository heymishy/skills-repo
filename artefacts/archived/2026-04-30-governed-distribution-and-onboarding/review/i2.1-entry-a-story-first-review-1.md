## FINDINGS

### 1-M1 — MEDIUM — AC2 is structurally redundant and not independently testable

AC2 states: "Given /orient has detected Entry A and the operator follows the routing to run /discovery, when the operator produces a discovery artefact and progresses to the inner loop, then the /orient routing output has explicitly surfaced /tdd as the implementation entry — the operator knows they can attach their existing story work to the /tdd step without re-deriving it from scratch."

This AC is not independently testable. Its Given clause requires multi-step operator actions (follow routing, produce a discovery artefact, progress to inner loop) that are outside the scope of what `/orient` outputs. The Then clause ("the /orient routing output has explicitly surfaced /tdd") restates what is already required by AC1(c): "names /tdd as the inner loop entry point for stories that are already well-understood." AC2 adds no new observable behaviour.

**Recommended action:** Remove AC2 as a standalone AC. Strengthen AC1(c) to specify the exact phrasing or explicitness required for the /tdd mention — e.g. "explicitly names `/tdd` as the inner loop entry point and states that existing story artefacts can be attached without re-derivation". This captures the intent of AC2 without creating an untestable multi-step AC.
