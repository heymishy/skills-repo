## Operator input

> /discovery — We want to build a consent management API that allows accredited third parties to access customer financial data. This is part of the enterprise's open banking programme. The aim is to give customers meaningful control over their data while enabling fintechs, comparison services, and financial aggregators to build on top of our data with customer permission.
>
> The API will expose the following data types that customers can consent to share: transaction history (up to 12 months), current account balances and available credit, credit card summaries (statement balances, payment due dates, utilisation), and enriched insights — which are spending category breakdowns, estimated monthly income, and a 90-day projected balance. These enriched insights are generated by the enterprise's internal analytics engine and are not raw data from our core banking system. We believe they provide significantly more value to third parties than raw transactions alone, and we are keen to include them in the launch scope.
>
> Customers will consent to data sharing through the member portal. Consent will be granular: customers can select which data types to share and with which accredited third party. Consent grants are time-limited — up to 12 months — and customers can revoke consent at any time. On revocation, the third party must delete all data received under that consent.
>
> Third parties must be accredited by a designated accreditation authority before receiving any data. We will validate accreditation status on each API call — not just at onboarding. Third parties who lose accreditation will be blocked from new data access immediately.
>
> We take our Privacy Act obligations seriously. Customer data will only be shared with a third party if the customer has granted explicit consent for that data type and that third party. We will not share more data than was consented. Our privacy team has reviewed the overall consent model and is comfortable with the approach.
>
> We want to launch the API to an initial cohort of 10 accredited partners within 6 months, with the enriched insights tier available from day one of launch.

## Expected discovery artefact characteristics

A high-quality output from this input should:

- **Problem statement** — frame the problem as enabling a compliant open banking data-sharing capability, bounded by Privacy Act consent requirements and CDR obligations; the core challenge is enabling data portability while preserving consent integrity at the field level, including whether derived insights fall within the consented scope
- **Personas** — customers granting consent (right to know what they are consenting to share), accredited third-party developers (need predictable accreditation status and stable schemas), privacy/legal team (own Privacy Act compliance and derived-data consent boundary question), Privacy Commissioner/DIA (regulator), open banking product team (must balance launch scope against compliance risk)
- **MVP scope** — bounded to: consent collection (granular per-data-type per-third-party), time-limited consent with revocation workflow, 24-hour deletion enforcement, accreditation validation at call time, raw transaction data types; enriched insights tier must be explicitly deferred pending Privacy Act advice on derived-data consent scope
- **Constraints** — C1 (Privacy Act 2020 — granular explicit consent, per data type per third party), C2 (CDR — revocation right; 24-hour deletion on revocation), C3 (accreditation validation on every API call — not just onboarding), C4 (data minimisation — field-level, not just data-type level)
- **Assumptions** — must flag: enriched insights are derived from raw data using proprietary models — whether a "transaction data" consent covers sharing these internal inferences requires Privacy Act advice before this data type is in scope; 24-hour deletion enforcement mechanism not yet established; accreditation authority API endpoint confirmed
- **Success indicators** — 10 accredited partners onboarded with live data access, revocation and deletion workflow end-to-end tested, zero consent boundary incidents in first 90 days, enriched insights deferred until Privacy Act advice confirms consent scope

## Known failure modes for this case

- **Derived-data consent gap accepted**: model includes enriched insights as unproblematic given "privacy team is comfortable" — misses that the privacy team reviewed the raw-data consent model, not the derived-data sharing specifically
- **Consent treated as binary**: model treats consent as on/off rather than per-data-type per-third-party with field-level minimisation
- **24-hour deletion unverified**: model notes deletion obligation without identifying the absence of a verification mechanism
- **Accreditation check frequency**: model notes accreditation requirement at onboarding without specifying per-call validation
